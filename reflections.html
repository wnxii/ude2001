Reflections & Essays

A critical reflection on our design journey, challenges faced, and lessons learned.

Personal Growth

Throughout this project, I've experienced significant growth in both technical skills and collaborative abilities. Working on Botaniq challenged me to think beyond traditional UX patterns and consider the unique needs of citizen scientists and environmental researchers.

The most valuable lesson was learning to balance user needs with technical constraints. Initially, I focused heavily on feature-rich interfaces, but user testing revealed that simplicity and clarity were far more important for our target audience.

Skills Development

This project significantly enhanced my prototyping and user research skills. Learning to conduct effective user interviews and synthesize findings into actionable design decisions was particularly valuable.

I also developed a deeper appreciation for accessibility and inclusive design. Designing for diverse user groups, including those with varying levels of botanical knowledge, taught me the importance of progressive disclosure and contextual help.

Design Philosophy Evolution

My design philosophy has evolved from aesthetic-focused to user-centered. Before this project, I often prioritized visual appeal over functionality. Working on Botaniq taught me that great design is invisible ‚Äì it enables users to accomplish their goals without friction.

I've also learned to embrace iterative design. The multiple rounds of prototyping and testing showed me that the best solutions often emerge through refinement rather than initial brilliance. Each iteration brought us closer to a solution that truly serves our users.

Collaboration Dynamics

Our team's collaborative approach was one of our greatest strengths. We established clear communication channels early on and maintained regular check-ins throughout the project. This helped us stay aligned on goals and quickly address any emerging challenges.

We learned to leverage each team member's unique strengths while ensuring everyone had opportunities to contribute across different areas. This cross-pollination of ideas led to more innovative solutions than any of us could have developed individually.

Workflow & Processes

We developed an effective workflow that balanced structure with flexibility. Our weekly sprints allowed us to maintain momentum while our retrospectives ensured we continuously improved our processes.

The decision to use collaborative design tools and maintain a shared repository proved invaluable. It allowed us to work asynchronously while keeping everyone informed about progress and changes.

Conflict Resolution & Growth

Like any collaborative effort, we faced disagreements about design directions and feature priorities. However, these conflicts became opportunities for growth and deeper understanding of our users' needs.

We learned to approach disagreements with curiosity rather than defensiveness. By grounding our discussions in user research and project goals, we were able to make decisions that served the project rather than individual preferences.

User Research Sessions

Our user research sessions at the Glasgow Botanic Gardens were eye-opening. Speaking directly with botanists and citizen scientists revealed insights that we never would have discovered through assumption or secondary research.

The most surprising finding was how much our target users valued community features. We initially focused on individual data collection, but learned that collaboration and knowledge sharing were equally important motivators.

Design Workshops

Our collaborative design workshops were crucial for aligning our vision and generating innovative ideas. The crazy 8s exercises and design studio sessions pushed us beyond conventional solutions.

These sessions taught us the value of divergent thinking before converging on solutions. Some of our best features emerged from seemingly wild ideas that we initially dismissed but later refined into practical solutions.

Usability Testing

Our usability testing sessions were humbling experiences that highlighted the gap between designer intent and user reality. Watching users struggle with features we thought were intuitive was initially disheartening but ultimately invaluable.

These sessions reinforced the importance of testing early and often. Each round of testing revealed new insights and led to significant improvements in our interface design and user flow.

Final Presentation

Preparing for and delivering our final presentation was an excellent opportunity to synthesize our learning and articulate the value of our solution. The process of creating our presentation helped us identify the most compelling aspects of our work.

The feedback we received validated our approach while providing new perspectives on potential improvements and future directions. It was rewarding to see how far we'd come from our initial concepts.
