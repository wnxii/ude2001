Prototyping Techniques

A comprehensive toolkit of prototyping methods for every stage of the design process

🔄

Iterative Prototyping

Sequential testing and refining of a single prototype through multiple cycles for gradual improvement.

Best for: Continuous improvement, requirement achievement

🧬

Evolutionary Prototyping

Continuous refinement where the prototype evolves into the final product rather than being discarded.

Best for: Long-term development, resource efficiency

⚡

Parallel Prototyping

Creating and testing multiple different concepts simultaneously to increase design diversity.

Best for: Exploring alternatives, reducing design fixation

📝

Paper Parallels

Team members each create paper prototypes and discuss them to generate iterative improvements.

Best for: Team brainstorming, rapid ideation

🏆

Competitive Prototyping

Separate teams independently work to solve the same problem, fostering high divergence and innovation.

Best for: Innovation challenges, exploring diverse solutions

📱

Wireframes

Simple, abstract layouts focusing on structure and functionality while ignoring visual design.

Best for: Layout planning, information architecture

📄

Paper Prototyping

Interactive mockups using paper and sticky notes where a person "plays computer" by swapping elements.

Best for: Early UI testing, quick iterations

🎬

Storyboarding

Comic strip-like sequences visualizing user interactions and journeys over time.

Best for: User journey mapping, context understanding

📦

Cardboard Prototyping

Physical mockups using cardboard - cheap, disposable, and easy to shape for tangible products.

Best for: Physical products, spatial relationships

🧱

LEGO Prototyping

Quick physical models using LEGO bricks to explore shape, size, functionality, and ergonomics.

Best for: 3D concepts, modular systems

🃏

Deck of Cards Prototyping

Each card represents components or actions that can be shuffled to simulate random sequences and edge cases.

Best for: Testing edge cases, random scenarios

🎭

Experience Prototyping

First-hand appreciation of system engagement through situational kits, physical proxies, or spatial mockups.

Best for: Understanding user emotions, contextual design

🎪

Role Playing

Acting out stakeholder roles to simulate service journeys and uncover pain points in user experiences.

Best for: Service design, stakeholder empathy

🏃‍♂️

Roleplay Relay

Structured version where actors pass users through service flows like relay races using scenario cards.

Best for: Complex service flows, handoff points

📺

Reality Show Prototyping

Scripted video scenarios with different personas to highlight use cases and make problems visible.

Best for: Stakeholder communication, problem illustration

🔄

Human GIF Prototyping

Team acts out short, looping interactions to demonstrate single UI features with people as interface elements.

Best for: Quick feature demos, interaction flows

🧙‍♂️

Wizard of Oz Prototyping

Users interact with seemingly functional interfaces while humans secretly operate the system responses.

Best for: AI features, complex backend testing

💻

Virtual Prototyping

Prototypes existing and tested on computational platforms using simulations and virtual environments.

Best for: Complex systems, cost-effective testing

🏗️

CAD-Based Rapid Prototyping

Detailed 3D models with virtual simulations followed by 3D printing or CNC fabrication.

Best for: Physical products, engineering validation

🥽

AR/VR Prototyping

Immersive simulations and augmented reality overlays for testing layouts and user interactions.

Best for: Spatial interfaces, immersive experiences

🔗

Hardware-in-the-loop

Connecting real physical components to computer simulations for realistic system performance testing.

Best for: IoT systems, embedded device testing

🤲

Haptic Prototypes

Tactile feedback prototypes providing force or vibration through haptic interfaces connected to virtual models.

Best for: Tactile interfaces, accessibility design

📏

Scaled Prototyping

Smaller or larger physical models that mimic final design behavior through principles of similitude.

Best for: Large systems, architectural models

💥

Disruptive Prototyping

Bold approach ignoring conventional wisdom to challenge status quo and redefine entire categories.

Best for: Innovation breakthroughs, market disruption

💭

Provotyping

Provocative prototypes based on extreme scenarios to provoke discussion and explore ethical implications.

Best for: Ethics exploration, societal impact discussion

🚀

Science Fiction Prototyping

Using narrative and storytelling to explore designs in various potential future scenarios.

Best for: Future planning, scenario exploration

🎲

Board Game Simulation

Converting app or service flows into physical board games to make abstract features tangible.

Best for: Gamification, complex system understanding

🔍

Subsystem Isolation

Prototyping and testing single parts of larger systems in isolation to reduce cost and focus effort.

Best for: Complex systems, targeted testing

🏙️

Street-to-Screen Prototyping

Physical mockups placed in real-world contexts for public testing before digital implementation.

Best for: Public services, community feedback
