Biodiversity Data Management Crisis in Glasgow

Limited Scale

Difficulty tracking city-wide trends

🔒
Underutilized Data

iNaturalist data not integrated

Glasgow Botanic Garden's Biodiversity Challenge

  • • Manual biodiversity tracking via traditional spreadsheets
  • • Single domain expert processing quarterly data
  • • Limited integration with citizen science platforms
📊 Key Stakeholders

  • • Domain Experts: Species identification specialists

Glasgow's commitment to environmental sustainability requires accurate, real-time biodiversity monitoring to inform urban planning decisions and conservation efforts across the city's green spaces.

Supporting Glasgow's Net Zero by 2030 goals and biodiversity action plans.

Research Insights

To validate our solution approach, we conducted extensive research on existing biodiversity platforms, focusing on iNaturalist as the leading community-driven biodiversity database.

🌍

Understanding iNaturalist

What is iNaturalist?

It's a global online platform where citizens, scientists, and nature enthusiasts share observations of plants, animals, and other organisms. Think of it as a "social network for nature" where people upload photos of wildlife they encounter.

The platform has grown to become one of the largest active online communities for biodiversity recording and information sharing, with millions of observations from around the world.

Why This Matters for Our Project:

iNaturalist proves that community-driven biodiversity data collection works at scale. We can use their approach as a guide and potentially integrate their data into our Glasgow-focused solution, giving us access to existing observations in the area.

🤖

AI Technology Implementation

Does iNaturalist use AI?

Yes. They make use of computer vision technology that can automatically suggest species identifications from photos.

Hybrid AI System:

Local Processing: Basic AI works offline on your device, providing instant suggestions even without internet

Cloud Enhancement: With access to internet, a more powerful cloud-based AI provides improved accuracy and can identify more species

88.3%

AI model accuracy rate

Covers over 1,000 species

Technical model details
Validation for Our Approach:

iNaturalist's success with computer vision AI proves that AI-assisted species identification is not only viable but highly effective. We can adopt similar technology for our Glasgow Botanic Garden solution.

📊

Data Quality & Reliability

How does iNaturalist ensure data accuracy?

They use a "research grade" system that requires community verification before data is considered scientifically reliable.

Community Verification Process:

At least 2 independent experts must agree on the species identification, with a minimum of ⅔ consensus required

Photo Quality Standards:

Images must be clear, geo-tagged with location data, dated, and show wild (not captive) organisms

95%

Research-grade accuracy rate

Official accuracy study
Implications for Our Solution:

With 95% accuracy, iNaturalist's data is scientifically trustworthy and suitable for integration into our application. This validates our approach of using community-contributed data with expert verification.

🔗

Implementation Pathway

How can we leverage iNaturalist?

We discovered that iNaturalist provides an API (Application Programming Interface) - essentially a way for our application to connect with and access their data.

✓ Data Reliability Confirmed

95% accuracy rate validates that iNaturalist data is scientifically sound and suitable for our application

✓ AI Technology Proven

88.3% accuracy demonstrates that computer vision for species identification is mature and effective

✓ API Access Available

Technical integration is feasible - we can pull Glasgow-area observations directly into our system

Research Conclusion

iNaturalist provides a solid, validated foundation for our biodiversity monitoring solution. Their proven success gives us confidence in our technical approach.

Key Research Takeaways
Proven Model

Quality Assured

Proposed Solution

AI-Powered Biodiversity Data Integration Platform

A comprehensive four-part system that revolutionizes Glasgow's biodiversity monitoring

1 Automated data collection via iNaturalist API
2 AI-assisted pre-screening for accuracy
3 Expert verification with active learning
4 Customized dashboards for all stakeholders
🤖 Automated Intelligence

ML models analyze images and predict species accuracy (e.g., "User: Foxglove | Model: Foxglove (92%)")

👩‍🔬 Expert Verification

Tinder-like interface for quick expert review with active learning that improves over time

📊 Decision Support

Real-time dashboards with AI alerts for decision-makers (e.g., invasive species tracking)

🌍 Public Engagement

Citizen-friendly portal with photo ID, location-based exploration, and gamification

Result: Transform quarterly manual processing into real-time, scalable biodiversity monitoring
